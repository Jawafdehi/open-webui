# Nepal Court Hierarchy

## Supreme Court (सर्वोच्च अदालत)

- The highest court in Nepal
- Located in Kathmandu
- Final appellate authority
- Hears constitutional cases, writ petitions, and appeals from High Courts
- Court Identifier: `supreme`

## Special Court (विशेष अदालत)

- Dedicated court for corruption and organized crime cases
- Hears CIAA-filed cases
- Located in Kathmandu
- Court Identifier: `special`

## High Courts (उच्च अदालत)

Seven High Courts serving different regions:

| Court | Identifier | Jurisdiction |
|-------|-----------|-------------|
| Biratnagar High Court | `biratnagarhc` | Province 1 eastern |
| Janakpur High Court | `janakpurhc` | Province 2 |
| Patan High Court | `patanhc` | Bagmati Province |
| Pokhara High Court | `pokharahc` | Gandaki Province |
| Tulsipur High Court | `tulsipurhc` | Lumbini Province |
| Surkhet High Court | `surkhethc` | Karnali Province |
| Dipayal High Court | `dipayalhc` | Sudurpashchim Province |

Extended benches:
- Ilam (`illamhc`), Dhankuta (`dhankutahc`), Okhaldhunga (`okhaldhungahc`)
- Rajbiraj (`rajbirajhc`), Birgunj (`birganjhc`)
- Hetauda (`hetaudahc`)
- Baglung (`baglunghc`)
- Butwal (`butwalhc`), Nepalgunj (`nepalgunjhc`)
- Jumla (`jumlahc`)
- Mahendranagar (`mahendranagarhc`)

## District Courts (जिल्ला अदालत)

- One per district (77 districts)
- Original jurisdiction for most civil and criminal cases
- Identifier format: district name + `dc`
- Examples: `kathmandudc`, `lalitpurdc`, `bhaktapurdc`
