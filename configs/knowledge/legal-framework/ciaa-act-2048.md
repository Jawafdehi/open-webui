# Commission for the Investigation of Abuse of Authority Act, 2048 (1991)

## Overview

The CIAA Act establishes the Commission for the Investigation of Abuse of Authority as Nepal's constitutional anti-corruption watchdog. The Act defines the CIAA's structure, powers, and procedures.

*Note: Full legal text to be sourced from the Nepal Law Commission and uploaded here.*

## Key Provisions

### Establishment (Sections 1-5)
- Constitutional basis (Article 238-239)
- Composition of the Commission (Chief Commissioner + Commissioners)
- Appointment and tenure
- Qualifications and disqualifications

### Functions and Duties (Sections 6-15)
- Investigate abuse of authority by public officials
- Investigate improper conduct and corruption
- Recommend departmental action
- File cases at the Special Court
- Annual reporting to the President and Parliament

### Powers (Sections 16-25)
- Summon witnesses and documents
- Search and seizure
- Arrest without warrant (in certain circumstances)
- Freeze assets
- Access to government records
- Contempt powers

### Procedures (Sections 26-35)
- Complaint registration
- Preliminary investigation (30-day timeline)
- Formal investigation (6-month timeline)
- Case filing at Special Court
- Withdrawal of cases

### Offenses (Sections 36-45)
- Obstruction of investigation
- False complaints
- Disclosure of confidential information
- Contempt of the Commission
